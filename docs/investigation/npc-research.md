# Tibia NPC Research: Bonelord 469 Cipher

Research compiled 2026-03-23. Sources: TibiaWiki (tibia.fandom.com), TibiaSecrets.com, TibiaQA, Tales of Tibia, GitHub s2ward/469.

---

## GEOGRAPHIC CORRECTION

**The Hellgate Library is NOT under Kazordoon.** It is located underground beneath **Ab'Dendriel** (the elf city in the north). Access route: Ab'Dendriel depot underground → Shadow Caves (NW) → down 2 floors → south then east → Hellgate door (requires Key 3012). Kazordoon is a separate dwarven city in the center of the continent. There is no known direct tunnel connection between Kazordoon and Hellgate in documented game lore.

### 3. Knightmare (NPC)
**Location:** Available at Tibia anniversary events / accessible in-game as an NPC (not the game developer — this is the NPC version)
**Why relevant:** Speaks a known 469 sequence; connects bonelords to Excalibug lore; hints that the player should ask about Excalibug in bonelord language.

**Key dialogue:**
- Randomly says: `"3478 67 90871 97664 3466 0 345!"`
- On Excalibug: "Minotaurs, ghouls and the then differently named bonelords were really dangerous for the heroes of that time."
- Key hint: "I'd tell you if you'd ask me in be ... uhm bonelord language." (suggests saying Excalibug in 469 may unlock a response)
- The sequence `3478` appears in both the Knightmare NPC's speech AND in the Honeminas Formula book in Demona library

**Research note:** `3478` decodes to BONELORD under the working cipher hypothesis (34=B, 78=E → further decoding). If `3478` = BONELORD, Knightmare is saying "Bonelord [something] [something]..." Try keyword `excalibug` spoken as its putative 469 encoding.

### 7. Honeminas (historical entity, referenced in book)
**Location:** Referenced in Demona Library book: "The Honeminas Formula"
**Why relevant:** A bonelord who lived with warlocks of Demona. Created mathematical formulas for understanding the Magic Web. The formula contains `(4,3,1,5,3)·(3,4,7,8,4)` — a dot product where `3478` appears, the same sequence as Knightmare's 469 speech.

**The formula:** `g[a_,x_] := a g[3,2] + (4,3,1,5,3)·(3,4,7,8,4)` where g=gate, e=energy

**Research note:** This is not an interactive NPC but a deceased bonelord referenced in a book. However, the NPC Zhandramon in Demona may have more to say about Honeminas. The digit sequence `(4,3,1,5,3)·(3,4,7,8,4)` may encode something significant to the cipher.

### 8. A Prisoner (The Mad Mage)
**Location:** Paradox Tower, near Kazordoon
**Why relevant:** Teaches "mathemagics" — the same term the Wrinkled Bonelord uses for the 469 language foundation. The Paradox Tower also contains a mirrored room with books potentially related to the cipher. There is a direct teleport from Paradox Tower to Hellgate.

**Key dialogue:**
- On mathemagics: "So know that everything is based on the simple fact that 1 + 1 is [1 or 13, 49, 94]" (the Tetranacci sequence: 1, 1, 3, 9, 4, which contains 4, 9 — cipher-relevant)
- On power: mentions "Aureus" who said "parcels are equal to power"
- Teaches the player a unique number as "the secret of mathemagics"

**Keywords:** `hi`, `math`, `yes`, `color` (player's color), `yes` → receives unique number and achievement "Mathemagician"
**Additional keywords to try:** `bonelord`, `language`, `469`, `formula`, `cipher`, `numbers`, `hellgate`, `library`

**Research note:** The 1+1 = 13, 49, 94 (Tetranacci) answer is mathematically significant and mirrors bonelord mathemagic concepts. The tower's mirrored room reportedly contains cipher-relevant books.

## KEY FACTS ABOUT 469 CIPHER (for NPC conversation context)

1. **Zero is forbidden**: The Wrinkled Bonelord calls 0 an "obscene number." This may mean valid cipher text never contains 0.
2. **The world is called "1"**: "It's 1, not 'Tibia'" — significant possible cipher word.
3. **Mathemagic**: The cipher is mathematical, not alphabetic substitution alone. 1+1 may equal 1, 13, 49, or 94 depending on Tetranacci rules.
4. **3478** = likely BONELORD (appears in Knightmare NPC speech and Honeminas formula; 3478 is also a Nostalgia Bonelord creature name in the 25th anniversary).
5. **Number pairs**: Left number is consistently ~1.5x the right number in cipher pairs.
6. **Spaces**: Written 469 in books has no spaces. Spoken 469 (NPCs) has spaces between sequences.
7. **4378 vs 3478**: Avar Tar's poem contains 4378 (reversed from the standard 3478) — possibly deliberate deception or a cipher variant.
8. **Hellgate books average**: All 71 Hellgate books yield numerical averages beginning with "4.something" — suggesting deliberate encoding structure.
9. **Identical books**: Isle of Kings library has a book identical to Hellgate Book 35. Ferumbras Citadel/Kharos also contains a 469 text with matching average.
10. **Paradox Tower connection**: The Mad Mage's mirrored room has books potentially relevant to the cipher; direct teleport to Hellgate exists from the tower.
