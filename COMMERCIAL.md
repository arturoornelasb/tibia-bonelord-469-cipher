# Commercial Participation

## Who needs a commercial license?

Any **for-profit company or commercial entity** using this software, techniques, or research in production.

**You do NOT need a commercial license if you are:**
- An individual (personal projects, freelancing, learning)
- An academic or research institution
- A non-profit organization
- A wiki or community site citing the research with attribution

All users — free or commercial — are subject to the contribution terms in [TERMS.md](TERMS.md). If you improve it, share it back.

## How it works

This project operates on a **consortium model**: companies that participate get full production rights, and their contributions are packaged and distributed back to all participants.

### What counts as participation

Commercial licenses are granted in exchange for one or more of:

| Contribution | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Engineering** | Bug fixes, features, new cipher attacks submitted as PRs |
| **Compute** | GPU hours, cloud credits, CI/CD infrastructure |
| **Data** | Additional cipher texts, transcription verification, NPC dialogue data |
| **Funding** | Direct sponsorship or subscription tiers |

The specific terms are negotiated per organization. There is no fixed price — participation scales with usage and organizational size.

### What participants receive

- Production license for all BUSL-1.1 repos in the ecosystem
- Validated, packaged releases with integration support
- Influence on the roadmap (prioritized features, compatibility)
- Early access to new tools and research

---

## Why this model

Cryptanalytic research and open-source tooling should be shared infrastructure — but building and maintaining it requires resources.

This model ensures:
1. **Individuals and researchers** use it freely, but share improvements back
2. **Companies** contribute resources proportional to the value they extract
3. **Everyone** benefits from a continuously improving, collectively funded toolkit
4. **No one** extracts value without giving back — the whole ecosystem grows together
